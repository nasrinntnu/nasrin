About Me

Accomplished Molecular Medicine researcher with expertise in vascular biology, circadian rhythm regulation, and endothelial dysfunction, focusing on circadian genes CLOCK and BMAL1 in cardiovascular disease.

I hold dual Master's degrees in Molecular Medicine and Biotechnology & Genetic Engineering, with peer-reviewed publications and international conference contributions. My research experience spans molecular biology, microbiology, public health, and machine learning–driven healthcare analytics.

I am experienced in siRNA-mediated gene knockdown, endothelial cell culture, angiogenesis assays, gene and protein expression profiling, and quantitative data analysis. My work has been recognized through multiple funded research projects and publications in prestigious venues.

I am motivated to pursue a PhD to advance translational biomedical research and uncover molecular mechanisms of cardiovascular pathophysiology.
